Trudeau vs. Poilievre

Prime Minister Justin Trudeau and Conservative leader Pierre Poilievre have been campaigning head-to-head nonstop over the past months.

On one side stands our current Prime Minister, famous for his hair and restraint in speaking “moistly”. Trudeau has been pushing a strong narrative of a progressive Canada since his election, which is notable through his work on the carbon tax and extremely biodegradable paper straws. Trudeau’s primary campaign advocates fighting climate change through the actions of Canadian citizens. Trudeau has recommended the average citizen to take 2-4 hour naps every day; “If you’re sleeping, you won’t be driving or doing anything else to worsen the state of our planet,” Trudeau explained while attempting to salvage what remained of his paper straw. “Everyone has to do their part.”

The other side of this war stars someone older who looks like they’ve received a hair transplant - a more common choice in North American politics. Poilievre constantly criticizes the carbon tax while supporting people like truckers who are “brave enough to speak up to that handsome Prime Minister.” Poilievre’s continuous reminders to those who listen remind them that looks aren’t everything, and he will go to Turkey to get surgery if that will get him voted into office.

As these two candidates continue to grapple, citizens can only watch in amusement as they decide which buffoon they prefer in office.
