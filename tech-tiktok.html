TikTok Ban in the USA

Popular social media platform TikTok is currently under fire from the U.S. government as the senator's niece's friend told her that “Tiktok is selling her information to some Chinese guy”.

This immediately instigated a hearing to further investigate this serious rumour. The government’s swift action has left many questioning the priorities of those they elected.

After a painfully repetitive congressional hearing, senior citizen U.S. senator Alan Barns is still skeptical of the CEO’s birthplace, as that seemed to be the hot topic of the hearing. Senator Barns, known for his suspicion of modern technology, spent the majority of the hearing fixated on whether Singapore was a part of China. Other members of congress can still be found trying to locate Singapore on a map- some mistakenly pointing to South America.

Overall, the hearing did not provide any new information indicating TikTok’s relationship with the Chinese government. The TikTok CEO repeatedly assured the committee he is not a spy, which seemed to only confuse them further, if not work against him. Despite the lack of evidence, the senators left the hearing convinced they made significant progress, although it remains unclear what progress that was. Upon their departure, a Satire Spectrum reporter was able to overhear a discussion regarding a follow-up hearing to address whether TikTok dances were a form of subliminal messaging.
