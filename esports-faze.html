FaZe Clan Goes Bankrupt

FaZe Holdings Inc. made an impressive debut in the stock market in July 2022, followed by a $1.7 million party in San Diego featuring the famous rapper Travis Scott. Members of FaZe clan - social media influencers and gamers, wore expensive streetwear and filmed videos for their respective followings. Even though Travis Scott’s performance was barely audible, FaZe CEO Lee Trink called the party a success, and claimed they even made money.

The saga of FaZe began with teenage founders gaining fame through Call of Duty trickshot videos. The company expanded to 127 online personalities, but turning millions of followers into profit couldn’t always be done, as FaZe members reported “Extravagant spending and excessive salaries” by their management team. Trink’s spending included luxury influencer mansions and swanky dinners. Unfortunately, even bringing Snoop Dogg onto the board with a $2.6 million stock deal couldn't save them.

Over the next two years, FaZe soon started to descend into chaos. Trink was fired in September amidst losses and plummeting shares, and was promptly replaced by CFO Christoph Pachler. By the end of the year, FaZe Clan was known for their controversies and layoffs, and reported a $48.7 million loss. Shares, which were initially expected to soar, ended up at a pitiful 18 cents in 2024.

Their public trading debut was clearly poorly timed as investors grew wary of such deals. They managed to scrape together $57.8 million but left fans mourning the loss of their original identity. FaZe, which once had potential, is now merely a cautionary tale of what happens when some kids on TikTok meet corporate greed.
