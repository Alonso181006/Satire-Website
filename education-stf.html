Saskatchewan Government Rejects STF Demands

Since February of 2024, the Saskatchewan Teachers Federation (STF) has been in conflict with the Saskatchewan government. This conflict stems from the fact that teachers believe they aren’t being fairly compensated for the work they do. They are hoping for quality of life improvements like classroom size reductions and larger wages. Unfortunately for them, the government isn’t so fast to give them what they want.

After talking to two teachers under the STF, we found out exactly what they want.

“Right now class sizes are getting out of control, and the government isn’t doing anything about it,” Says Alonso Bastidas. “I teach an English class with 73 kids!”

Alonso is hoping that class sizes will be reduced to a maximum of 65. He, along with others, hopes that the government will agree to this change.

Another teacher, Max Liu, talked to us about wages. “All we want is a 0.002% increase to our income”, he explains. Right now teachers make between 10-12 thousand dollars a year, so this raise would allow for an extra $20 per year. The government argues they just don’t have the budget for such a large wage increase for all 13500 teachers.

Hopefully, in the near future, the government and teachers of Saskatchewan can come to an agreement.
