Quebec Hates Anglophones

It's amazing how the APAC region finally made history with a victory in the most recent Valorant tournament—Masters Shanghai. Gen.G, the South Korean team, pulled off an impressive win against Team Heretics from Europe, bringing home the first international trophy for the region. It's about time someone from the Pacific League stepped up and took the championship.

It's been quite a journey for the APAC region, with teams consistently performing well but never quite sealing the deal when it mattered. The whole military enlistment obstacle added an extra layer of drama, with players miraculously experiencing "health issues" that prevented them from completing their service. I mean, who wouldn't have health issues if they played Valorant 24/7, right?

Let's not forget Gen.G's loss to Sentinels at Masters Madrid, where they had the upper hand but fell short in the finals. It's almost comical how they lost to a team focused on selling bundles and content. Don’t even get me started on "Zellsis" and his performance. A guy who got fewer kills in a game than the times he said, “Buy the Sen Bundle,” and then failed to qualify for Masters Shanghai. At least they finally did win not because Dominykas “Miniboo” Lukaševičius was stuck at home finishing final exams, and Team Heretics was left playing with a substitute, but because GENG is the best team in the world.
