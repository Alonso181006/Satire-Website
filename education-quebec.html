Quebec Hates Anglophones

In fall of 2024, Quebec is close to doubling their tuition for students originating outside of the province. Quebec’s intent with this is an attempt to reduce the English-speaking population within the province, sparking controversy and outrage from students, parents, and educational institutions.

As further evidence to the extent Quebec will take this, francophones from Belgium are not subject to this tuition increase. This exemption shows the attempt Quebec is making to maintain diversity within the francophone population, instead of simply administering a French proficiency test.

“This is not about discrimination, but preservation of our culture. We also hate the anglophones that come here to enjoy our culture, but end up washing it out,” a government official stated.

“It’s like they’re building a money wall around the province!” a concerned parent declared. “It's ridiculous that my daughter can’t even attend with normal tuition if she learns French!”

University administrators expressed concerns about the impending detrimental impact this policy could have on enrollment numbers. “I can’t understand why they won’t administer a language test instead of blocking out everybody not from Quebec,” one particularly stressed administrator expressed while squeezing his poutine pop-it.

As Quebec prepares for this controversial tuition increase, the province must brace for the increasing backlash as well. Despite the intention of preserving linguistic heritage, accusations of inclusivity, economic discrimination and fears for the uncertain future of post-secondary education in Quebec are in discussion. The long-term impacts of this policy will have to be seen with time.
