Calculus Students Parody Popular Song Instead of Studying

AP classes are courses that challenge students at a college level understanding. Provided a student performs well enough on an AP test, they can earn college credit for their efforts. Intuitively, AP Calculus is just like any other AP class, with the subject matter pertaining to Calculus.

On Monday May 13, 2024, students from all around the globe wrote the AP Calculus test. Roughly 50 of these students took this test at Evan Hardy Collegiate Institute in Saskatoon, Saskatchewan. Following the completion of the test, these students were rewarded with 7 “earned days off”, where they didn’t have to come to calculus class for the duration.

Unfortunately, upon their return to class on the 23rd of May, the class found out that the last month of school would be wasted. The students were given two assignments. The first: a pitiful booklet with the condition “As long as you get 50% of the questions right, I won’t change your grade,” outlines their teacher Brad Jorgenson. The second assignment is even worse: a calculus themed parody song which the students don’t even have to complete to earn full credit for the class.

We talked to a student to hear his thoughts on the matter.

“I just want to learn more math!” Will Hlady exclaims. “I love writing tests.”

Will believes there is no such thing as days off in math. “Even when we weren’t supposed to come to class, I sometimes snuck into the back room to practice integrals”.

Considering the teaching style of Mr. Jorgenson, we can clearly see why the Saskatchewan Government doesn’t want to fund its teachers.
