Elon Musk - Is Neuralink Out of control?

Recently, both tech enthusiasts and conspiracy theorists have found a common ground when it comes to Elon Musk’s Neuralink. Neuralink is a chip that is implanted into the brains of humans, and it has officially gone rogue. Initially, the product showed promise; it had intent to treat neurological conditions and even had the potential to augment human capabilities. Those days are long over though, as this technology has taken an unexpected turn.

Musk’s whole project started innocently enough. Early investors were happy to indulge in Neuralink because of how much promise it had. Unfortunately, upon the first few hundred thousand tests of the project, things got a little bit too connected. Users of Neuralink reported their brains picking up Wi-Fi signals, which led to spontaneous Google searches for cat videos during meetings and sometimes the urge to order pizza at 3 AM. One man even claimed his implant had him “binge watching soap operas in his sleep”.

The main cause behind these anomalies turned out to be a group of hackers named the “NeuroNerds”. They began infiltrating minds and inflicting rather harmless pranks on their victims. One person said they had thoughts of “spontaneous dance routines” and “uncontrollable fits of karaoke”. Government officials worldwide scrambled to try to counter this new form of cyberterrorism, but not before three world leaders were seen doing the Macarena.

Elon Musk assured that Neuralink’s issues were akin to a baby “just teething”. He announced a new update called “Brain Shield”, in order to prevent further incidents. Musk also introduced Neuralink 2.0 in order to promise greater integration with AI. What could go wrong?
